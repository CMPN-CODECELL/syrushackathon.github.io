SYRUS 2019 :

Syrus hackathon focused on promoting innovation, diversity and networking among all future hackers. Upcoming engineers and developers were hosted for an intense 34-hour session. Problem statements were mainly focused on social issues and transformation with the help of ICT and technology. The fundamental domains were agriculture, health care, education, governance, energy, transport and security.

MENTORS/JUDGES :

The judging panel consisted of college alumni along with Mr. Ali Mustafa, an Educator from Google Crowdsource India. Mr. Harsh Gupta (2017 batch) and Ms. Padmaja Kole (2017 batch) were present on the first day, while Mr. Nandan Doddihal (2016 batch) and Mr. Parag Shah (2006 batch) were present on the second day. Alongside them, the college faculty consisting of Prof. Mr. Prashant Kanade, Prof. Mrs. Priya R. L. and Prof. Mr. Richard Joseph provided their valuable input and acted as mentors to the participants.

WINNERS OF 2019 :

The winners of the 2019 Syrus Hackathon were team AWM comprising of Saket Ozarkar, Sugam Devare and Sumeet Haryani. The team had selected the domain as Education. After a rigorous consideration the team was selected as the winner by a panel of judges. The team received a cash prize as well as various merchandise. Along with the main winners there were hourly quiz competitions winners of which received exciting prizes and goodies.
